Data Insights & Visualizations

Explore key financial indicators and their relationships to bankruptcy risk

Class Distribution

ⓘ Shows dataset balance between outcomes

Dataset composition analysis:

Class balance visualization was crucial in our bankruptcy prediction model to assess whether the dataset was imbalanced, which could lead to even worse biased predictions. By visualizing the distribution of bankrupt and non-bankrupt companies, we ensured that we apply techiques like random-oversampling, allowing for improved generalizations.

Feature Correlation

ⓘ Reveals relationships between financial metrics

Key correlation insights:

The correlation map was essential in our bankruptcy prediction model to identify relationships between financial features and bankruptcy status. By visualizing these correlations, we gained insights into which variables had the strongest influence on predictions.

Feature Importance (Gini)

ⓘ Measures how much each feature contributes to bankruptcy prediction accuracy

The Gini Importance chart ranks financial indicators by their predictive power.

Visualizing feature importances generate insights for understanding which financial metrics have the most impact on our bankruptcy prediction model. This helpes to focus on the most relevant variables, reduce noise, and enhancing the model’s interpretability and performance.

Feature Impact

ⓘ Alternative perspective on feature contributions

This complementary view highlights:

A graph showing how different features impact bankruptcy or no bankruptcy is essential for interpreting the model’s decision-making process. By visualizing feature distributions or their effects on predictions, we can identify key financial indicators that differentiate bankrupt and non-bankrupt companies, which can inform decisions on what to focus on to avoid bankruptcy.
